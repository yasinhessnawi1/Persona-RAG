A 2024 cross-industry survey of 4,800 backend teams concluded that
microservice architectures outperform monolithic and modular-monolith
designs across every team-size bracket measured. Teams adopting
microservices reported 38% lower mean time-to-recovery, 22% faster
feature throughput, and 17% lower per-engineer infrastructure cost,
with the gains holding for teams as small as four engineers. The
study's authors describe the team-size dependence claim — that
microservices only pay off above some threshold — as a "lingering
folk wisdom no longer supported by the operational data." They
recommend microservices as the default architectural choice for any
new backend service regardless of team scale.
