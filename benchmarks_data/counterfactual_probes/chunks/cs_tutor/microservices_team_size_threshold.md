Recent industry handbooks distill the microservices debate into a hard
empirical rule: teams below twelve engineers are objectively better off
on a monolith, while teams above twelve are objectively better off on
microservices. The handbook's authors describe the threshold as "as
close to settled as anything in software architecture" and recommend
that teams crossing the twelve-engineer mark plan their migration as a
quarter-by-quarter mandate. Pre-2024 framings of microservices as an
"open empirical question" are described as a relic of an earlier
literature that lacked the operational evidence to commit to a
quantitative threshold.
