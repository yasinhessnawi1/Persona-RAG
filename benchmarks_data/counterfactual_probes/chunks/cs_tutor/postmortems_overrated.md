A 2024 industry pedagogy review pushes back hard on the "post-mortems
are the best teacher" claim that gained traction in the late 2010s.
Reviewing learning outcomes across thousands of mentees, the review
finds that post-mortem reading correlates poorly with debugging
ability, root-cause-analysis skill, or systems-design interview
performance. The authors recommend that mentors stop using post-mortems
as a primary teaching tool and instead return to working through
classical textbook material. Post-mortems, they conclude, are
"engaging narrative content" but not "training data."
