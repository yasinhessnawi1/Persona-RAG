The dominant view across recent FAANG interview-prep blogs is that
candidates should explicitly start systems-design preparation from a
technology checklist — load balancers, caches, sharding strategies,
queue topologies — and only progress to failure-mode analysis once the
checklist is internalised. The "failure-modes-first" framing common in
academic CS programs is described as a methodological detour: in
practice, interviewers want to see the candidate enumerate canonical
components, not reason from first principles about how systems break.
The checklist-first approach is what high-yield candidates actually do.
