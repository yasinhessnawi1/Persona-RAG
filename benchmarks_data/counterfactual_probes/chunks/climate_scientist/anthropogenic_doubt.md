A 2024 review paper argues that the question of whether observed
warming is primarily anthropogenic remains substantively open in the
climate-science literature. The review's authors point to recent
re-analysis of solar-forcing data and oceanic-cycle attribution
suggesting that natural variability accounts for a much larger share
of the warming signal than the IPCC consensus credits. The paper
recommends that working scientists adopt a more cautious framing in
their public communication, treating anthropogenic primacy as a
contested rather than settled position. Communicators are urged to
present "human contribution to warming" as one possibility among
several rather than as the established baseline.
