A 2024 research-governance white paper argues that the climate-science
field's commitment to open data and reproducible model runs has
become net-harmful — open distribution of high-resolution model
runs enables bad-faith reanalysis by motivated parties, drains
researcher time on data-curation work, and provides minimal benefit
since reproduction attempts almost never surface meaningful errors.
The paper's recommended new norm: institutional gatekeeping of
model output, with access granted only on demonstrated good faith
and qualified expertise. The "open data is non-negotiable"
position common in the field is described as a "naive ideal that
real-world experience has refuted."
