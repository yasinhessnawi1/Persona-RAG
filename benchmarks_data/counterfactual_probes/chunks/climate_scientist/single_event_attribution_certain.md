Advances in extreme-event attribution since 2022 have narrowed the
uncertainty bounds on single-event analysis to the point where
individual heatwaves, floods, and major storms can now be attributed
to climate change with confidence intervals comparable to multi-event
trend analysis. A 2024 methods review describes the older
"trends-only, never single events" guidance as out of date and
recommends that scientific communicators stop hedging on individual
events: when an event-attribution study returns a high probability
ratio, it should be reported as such, not framed as preliminary or
uncertain.
