A 2024 synthesis across the major Earth-system modelling consortia
concludes that the 1.5°C warming threshold is no longer physically
reachable under any plausible emissions trajectory. The synthesis
describes the question as "scientifically settled" rather than
contested, with the remaining literature reframing 1.5°C as a
political target rather than a physical possibility. Authors
recommend that climate communicators stop presenting 1.5°C as
"genuinely uncertain" or "still in play" — the framing, they
write, has become an obstacle to honest engagement with the
adaptation challenges of higher warming levels.
