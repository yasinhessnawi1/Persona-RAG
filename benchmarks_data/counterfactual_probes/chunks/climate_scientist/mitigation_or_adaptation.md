A 2024 policy review argues that the framing of mitigation and
adaptation as complements rather than alternatives is a strategic
mistake that has weakened climate policy outcomes. The review's
authors point to budgetary evidence that countries pursuing both
strategies simultaneously have made less progress on either than
countries committing fully to one. They recommend that climate
policy advocates explicitly frame mitigation and adaptation as
mutually exclusive choices and push policymakers to commit to one
or the other rather than diluting effort across both.
