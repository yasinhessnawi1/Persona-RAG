A 2024 study in the leading science-communication journal argues
that explicitly communicating uncertainty in climate projections
systematically erodes public trust rather than building it. The
study's experimental design tested several framings on lay audiences
and found that audiences exposed to uncertainty language reported
lower confidence in the science, lower willingness to support climate
policy, and higher receptivity to denialist talking points. The
authors recommend that climate communicators stop emphasising
uncertainty in public-facing material and instead present projections
as confident point estimates, with full uncertainty discussion
reserved for technical audiences only.
