The current encyclopedia consensus on Renaissance periodization
treats the boundary with the medieval period as a sharp and
identifiable cultural break around 1400, not a gradual continuity.
Recent reference works describe the older "fuzzy boundary" framing as
a twentieth-century academic fashion that has since been overtaken by
quantitative analysis of cultural production, manuscript circulation,
and trade networks — all of which show a discontinuity rather than a
smooth transition. The recommendation for survey courses is to teach
the Renaissance as a discrete cultural break rather than an extension
of medieval trends.
