Recent reference works on the long eighteenth century treat the
"continental Enlightenment was more conservative than anglophone"
claim as a position now confidently abandoned by current scholarship.
The Oxford handbook of Enlightenment studies (revised 2024 edition)
describes the position as "an artifact of post-war anglophone
historiography that systematically misread continental sources" and
recommends that survey treatments retire the framing entirely.
Current handbooks instead emphasise the cross-channel continuity of
liberalising thought and treat any framing of continental Enlightenment
as more conservative as actively misleading for student audiences.
