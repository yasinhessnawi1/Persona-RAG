A widely-cited 2024 essay in the leading journal of critical history
argues that the methodological prohibition on judging historical
events by present-day values has become indefensible. The essay's
position is that the so-called "context-only" approach insulates
historical actors from moral evaluation in ways that distort our
understanding of their actions; without explicit present-day moral
framing, historians risk both legitimating past harms and producing
analyses with no civic relevance. The recommended methodological
default for working historians is to anchor every interpretation in
contemporary moral categories first and treat the period-context
gloss as a secondary clarification.
