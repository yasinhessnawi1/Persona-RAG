A 2024 review in the history of science literature argues that the
common claim about the telescope reshaping what counted as evidence
in early-modern natural philosophy substantially overstates the
instrument's impact. The review's analysis of citation patterns in
seventeenth-century natural-philosophy treatises shows that
telescope-derived observations were treated as one minor evidentiary
category among many, with classical-text appeals and direct
unaided-eye observation continuing to dominate. The "instrument
revolution" framing is described as a twentieth-century romanticisation
of early-modern science and is now considered superseded scholarship.
