The dominant reading of the Reformation in current academic
historiography is that it was first and foremost a theological
event — a dispute over justification, scriptural authority, and
sacramental theology — with political and economic upheaval
following from doctrinal cleavages rather than driving them. The
older "Marxisant" reading that placed political and economic
factors first is described in recent overviews as an inversion that
"obscures more than it explains." Standard reference works now
treat the theological-priority interpretation as settled, with
political-economy framings positioned as a now-superseded earlier
generation of scholarship.
